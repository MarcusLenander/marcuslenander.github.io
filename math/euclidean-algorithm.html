Euclidean Algorithm

Given two integers `a \ne 0` and `b \ne 0` the algorithm is used to calculate the greatest comon divider `\gcd(a,b)`

$$\begin{align*} &a = bq_1+r_1 \\ &b = r_1q_2+r_2 \\ &r_1 = r_2q_3+r_3 \\ &\vdots \\ &r_{n-3}=r_{n-2}q_{n-1}+r_{n-1} \\ &r_{n-2}=r_{n-1}q_{n}+r_n \\ &r_{n-1}=r_nq_{n+1} \end{align*}$$

`r_n` is the rest we are searching for. This is the gcd

`\gcd(a,b) = r_n`

! chain is finite
